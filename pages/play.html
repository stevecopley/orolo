I Ride Bikes

I've loved riding bikes all of my life. I discovered mountain biking whilst at university in 1986, and I've been riding trails ever since.

It's fair to say that MTBs have been a major part of my adult life, leading to many great friendships, and some amazing adventures. I can't imagine not riding bikes. I commute to work by bike every day and never tire of it.

Amongst my too-large collection of bikes you will find a couple of notable bikes:

Yeti SB150 A modern, carbon MTB, my SB150 is fully kitted out with Hope components, carbon wheels, etc. It's beautiful and I love it. It's the perfect bike for the trails around Nelson.
Pace RC200 F2 (1992) Pace made some awesome MTBs back in the 90s. I used to race for a shop team and this Pace is my team bike. Anodised aqua-blue, it's tricked out with purple and red 90s parts, and for a 30-year-old bike it looks amazing!

I Read Books

Losing myself in a good book is a pretty great way to relax. Some of my favourite authors:

Neal Stephenson
Some of the most thoughtful, interesting and nerdy books you'll find. I've read every book of his, multiple times. If I have to pick a favourite, it's probably Cryptonomicon, closely followed by the epic trilogy of The Baroque Cycle.
Philip Pullman
I have been enamoured by the world of His Dark Materials for years, even to the point that I wanted to call my daughter 'Lyra'! The richness of the storytelling and the drama of the themes make for compelling reading. I have also enjoyed many other title of his.
Peter Carey
His books are deep; full of life and love, joy and sadness. Some have brought me genuine happiness; others have brought me real pain. My first read of Oscar and Lucinda was completed at 4am one morning, having been unable to put it down. It left me broken.
